Unit 1.1 Describing the purposw of IT in modern buisiness.

Information Technology is a vital and integral part of modern day business, it is used from multinational corporations to small businesses that only have access to one computer. It is even used in something as hands on as agriculture. Farmers use computers for production record, financial planning, research on technical issues and procurement. With the introduction of computers, the business world has changed forever, companies use information technology to ensure that their departments run smoothly and IT is used in a wide range of departments including human resources, finance, manufacturing and security. Some of the main advantages to IT are a ride in productivity, better communication and improvements in data management.

Information technology helps in developing automated processes for businesses and helps in reducing the cost of operations. This has caused a complete shift of how companies spend their time. Time management has been optimized, and the effort that has been put into everyday tasks has been lightened. This has increased employee productivity allowing employees to focus more important things.

One of the main advantages of IT in modern business is it is now easier than ever before to communicate on a global scale. A good example of this are applications like Skype, Zoom and GoToMeeting allows companies to collaborate virtually in real time from multiple different locations which can reduce costs for companies that would otherwise have to bring clients on site or who have staff who work remotely. This saves time and increases productivity as it cuts travel time down. Customer communication has also been improved

In the past Data Management and storage had to be stored in a written form which would not only take time but those files had to be stored somewhere using up valuable space. Thanks to Data Management Systems this is a thing of the past companies can store Terabytes of data whilst taking up no physical space. Data Management Systems also don’t require users to regularly and manually back up as they are automatically in case of a crash whereas a written documentation would be lost if it was damaged [1].

The General Data Protection Regulations was created in 2016 and implemented into the EU on May 25th 2018. The GDPR objective is to adjust the legal regulations concerning the processing and protection of personal data. This gives the data subjects, a user of online service more control over the processing of their data. Some examples are;

The right to access

This allows an individual to request access to their personal data and how the company has been using their data after it’s been gathered.

The right to be forgotten

If a consumer is no longer a customer, or withdraw their consent from a company to use their personal data, then they have the right to have their data deleted.

The right to data portability

Individuals have the right to transfer their right from one service provider to another.

The right to be informed

An individual must be informed before data is gathered. A consumer has to opt in for their data to be gathered, and consent must be given freely rather than implied.

The right to have information correctedThe right to have information corrected

The individual has the right to have their data updated if it is out of date or incorrect.

The right to restrict processing

An individual can request their data is not used for processing, a record will be kept but can’t be used.

The right to object

This gives the individual the right to stop the processing of their data for direct marketing. This rule cannot be broken and must stop as soon as the request is received.

The right to be notified -

If there has been a data breach that has compromised an individual's personal data, the individual has a right to be informed within 72 hours of the breach [4]
